Discover the Land of the Bounty Ship Mutineers

On an initial mission to collect breadfruit trees, following a voyage filled with disrespect and punishments Fletcher Christian and his fellow mutineers seized the HMS Bounty on 1789. Along with their Tahitian companions they embarked on their journey circling the Pacific ocean in hope to find a safe hideout. After several months they found the remote tiny unhabited island of Pitcairn set fire to the Bounty and thus became ever since one the world's most isolated community Read more

Meet their descendents and discover their lifestyle and culture.

Relying on hard work, making the best out of natural resources Pitcairners have learnt how to survive in this harsh isolated environment for centuries. Mastering fishing, sailing, trading their crafts and fruits with passing ships made it all possible in sustaining the community.
