St Paul's Pool

One of the first and often last 'must see' natural attractions on Pitcairn is the incredibly beautiful St Paul's Pool. Situated on the eastern side of the island its pristine waters and inspiring cathedral like rock structures are at their best before early afternoon, when the sun is on the water. It looks just like an Olympic-sized natural pool of crystal blue water surrounded by a corona of basalt rocks with pinnacles. The walk there, being entirely downhill, is so typical of Pitcairn's natural tracks which enable happy wanderers to stop and collect bananas, coconuts and even avocados along the way.

As you near the rugged perimeter of the eastern side of the island above St Paul's the landscape gives way to broad spans of volcanic red dirt and rock which provide sustenance to prickly pears and Pandanus palms, giving the entire area a desert like feel. Then, just as you think there could be nothing more to see, you walk right up to the safety rail and take in the startling beauty of the pool. So clear that the ocean's green and blue lights seem to flicker like diamonds across its surface. It's incredibly inviting but there's still a little more walking… or in fact rock climbing… to get there. The last short trek must be negotiated with care.

Make sure to check on the weather conditions before you step into the pool, When the see is rough the wave can draw you away into the ocean violently.
Rock Climbing
Pitcairn being itself a huge volcanic rock it offers many climbing opportunities for the experienced climbers. Due to its remote location not many areas have been scouted and new spots are yet to be discovered. climbing on the cliffs over the ocean is indeed a unique experience which comes with a challenge, safety is always at risk so be aware of your limits and ask the locals about weather conditions. Pitcairners are all good climbers and most of them don't even wear shoes, don't make that mistake as some cliffs are razor sharp and can seriously injure you. One of the best known spots is St Paul's pool, where you can challenge the pinnacles over the ocean's surf. Another good spot would be the "down rope" cliff.
