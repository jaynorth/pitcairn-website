Adamstown and around activities

Many activities are essential to everyday living, but you'll always find time for leisure

Diving >>
Explore the wreck of the HMS Bounty and the Cornwallis, discover nearby islands underwater world. Diving opportunities are plentiful and many spots are yet to be discovered.

Christian's Cave >>
Fletcher Christian's cave, past the school and further up, is where the lead mutineer is said to have watched for approaching ships and or hid from his ruthless fellow settlers when necessary. Read More
St Paul's pool & Rock Climbing >>
Pitcairn Island is itself a big rock, it offers many challenging cliffs and climbing over the ocean water makes it a unique feature.
Read More
Fishing >>
Pitcairn's pristine waters ensure the fish are plentiful and delicious. Book a fishing trip with a local business or participate in a traditional community fishing trip. Read More
Nearby islands trip >>
The nearest islands of Henderson, Oeno and Ducie can be visited if the weather permits. All unhabited and unspoiled by humans, witness endemic species of creatures and plants.
Read More
Down Rope >>
Down Rope is a steep cliff on the southeast edge of the island, it has ancient Polynesian petroglyphs in its face and an isolated sandy beach at its base. Read More
